Wiring & Harness

Electrical Design: HV Battery Pack Wiring · Concordia Formula Racing

I designed the wiring architecture for the 369.6 V high voltage battery pack, defining every power and signal connection in and out of the pack, then routing, building, and verifying the harness within tight packaging constraints.

  • Routed all pack wiring for minimum length and resistance, and to keep the harness serviceable within the enclosure's spatial constraints
  • Maintained physical separation between high voltage and low voltage runs to reduce coupling/EMI and meet FSAE isolation requirements
  • Integrated the safety circuit (interlocks, the shutdown loop, and fault detection paths) so the tractive system shuts down correctly on any fault
  • Selected wire gauge, insulation rating, and connectors against each circuit's current, voltage, and thermal demands rather than a one size default
  • Verified continuity and isolation of the completed harness through systematic point to point testing before the pack was energized
  • Designed to the FSAE electrical ruleset throughout (HV isolation, conductor sizing, and connector requirements)
